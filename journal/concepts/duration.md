# Duration

**Last updated:** 2026-04-03

## Definition

How long you intend to hold an investment. Not just a time preference — it's a strategic variable that interacts with position sizing, compounding, and forward-return discipline.

## Key Insight

Duration is a valid alpha source, but **only as a byproduct** of two other things working together:
1. Concentration in high-quality compounders (Enright path 2)
2. Continuous reassessment of forward returns without anchoring to entry price (Enright path 3)

Duration alone — just not selling — is index-adjacent. The compounding math only works as alpha if the forward return from current price continues to beat alternatives.

## The Hold vs. Sell Framework

**Hold when:** From today's price, the forward return on the compounding business still beats alternatives. Entry price is irrelevant.

**Sell when:** The market re-rates the compounder (e.g., P/E goes from 15x to 30x), effectively pulling forward 5-7 years of returns. At that point, forward returns no longer justify holding even though the business is still excellent.

**The hard part:** Commitment/consistency bias (Munger/Cialdini) fuses your identity to the position. You hold because of who you are ("I'm a META shareholder") rather than what the math says.
