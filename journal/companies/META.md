# META Platforms

**Last updated:** 2026-04-03
**Current view:** Own it. Compounder with multiple Cialdini moats reinforcing each other.

## Thesis

META is a Lollapalooza business — multiple weapons of influence (social proof, commitment/consistency, scarcity of attention, network effects) compound simultaneously rather than operating independently. This makes the moat wider than any single-framework analysis suggests.

### Moat Assessment
- **Demand-side scale (network effects):** 3.3B+ DAP across family of apps. Each new user makes the platform more valuable for every existing user. This is social proof weaponized at infrastructure scale.
- **Switching costs:** Commitment/consistency bias locks users in — years of photos, connections, group memberships, Messenger history create identity fusion with the platform. Switching means abandoning accumulated social capital.
- **Attention scarcity:** Reels/short-form video captures finite attention budget. Once attention is allocated, it's zero-sum — time on META is time not on competitors.
- **Advertiser lock-in:** Advertisers face their own switching costs — pixel data, custom audiences, campaign optimization history, institutional knowledge of META's ad tools.

### Cialdini Layer (what most analysts miss)
The moat isn't just structural — it's psychological. Users don't stay because of network effects alone. They stay because:
- Social proof: "Everyone I know is here"
- Commitment/consistency: "I've invested years of my life in this platform"
- Liking: Curated feeds optimized to show content from people you like
- Scarcity: Fear of missing out on social information

These weapons combine multiplicatively (Lollapalooza), not additively.

- Reality Labs capex: If ROIIC on this investment stays below WACC, it's a massive value destroyer. Management's time horizon may not match shareholders'.
- Regulatory: Antitrust, data privacy, content moderation — ongoing structural risk.
- AI commoditization: If AI tools commoditize ad targeting, META's data advantage narrows.
- Generational shift: Younger users may not form the same commitment/consistency bonds if they start on different platforms.
